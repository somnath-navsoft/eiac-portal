  1. FIRST PARTY OBLIGATION
    1. CAB hereby confirms that the information provided in this agreement is valid true and correct .
    2. CAB acknowledges and agrees to abide and sustain full compliance at all times with the following documents relevant to the accredited work scope under this agreement and stated within the accreditation application:
      1. Relevant accreditation criteria, Accreditation requirements and Guidelines issued by EIAC and any Relevant Mandatory and Guidance Documents issued by the international Recognition Bodies. CAB is required to frequently visit the EIAC’s website www.eiac.gov.ae for the latest updates,
      2. committed to fulfill continually the requirements of accreditation scope for which accreditation is sought or granted and to commit to provide evidence of fulfillment . This includes agreement to adapt to changes in the requirements for accreditation,
      3. To provide EIAC with all needed documentation information as listed in the accreditation application
    3. To provide EIAC with clear and valid reasons for the objection to any member of the Assessment Personnel in writing within 5 (five) working days of receipt of the notification of Assessment .
    4. To arrange the witnessing of conformity assessment services when requested by EI AC,
    5. To have, where applicable, legally enforceable arrangements with their clients that committ he clients to provide, on request , access to EIAC’s assessment teams to assesst he CAB’s performance carrying out conformity assessment activities at the client’s site,
    6. To claim accreditation only with respect to the scope for which it has been granted accreditation,
    7. To commit to follow EIAC’s policy for the use of the accreditation symbol ,
    8. Not to use its accreditation in such a manner as to bring the EIAC into dispute,
    9. To assist in the investigation and resolution of any complaints made by third parties about the CAB’s accredited activities, and
    10. To inform EIAC, without delay, of significant changes relevant to its accreditation, in any aspect of its status or operation relating to :
      1. its legal , commercial , ownership or organizational status,
      2. the organization, top management and key per sonnel ,
      3. resources, premises and methods,
      4. scope of accreditation, and
      5. Other such matters that may affect the ability of the CAB to fulfill requirements for accreditation.
    11. The CAB is obliged to comply with EIAC Fees requirement available at website, knowing that all fees are non- refundable.
    12.     13. In case t he CAB del ays to conf or m t he assessment plan on defined time; all expenses, such as, but not limited to , travel tickets, accommodations and accreditation fees of the visit shall be paid in full .
    14. The CAB may withdraw from the present contractual agreement at any moment , by means of an of ficialletter sent to EIAC.
    15. Upon suspension or reduction of its accreditation (however determined) , CAB shall discontinue immediately with no delay the use of all presentations and advertising that contains any reference to that terminated scope of accreditation
    16. The CAB shall not cede, assign or in any way transfer its accreditation to another party and such status shall not extend to the franchisee or franchisor of the CAB
  2. EIAC OBLIGATION & RESPONSIBILITIES
    1. EIAC shall publish all updated documents on its website: www.eiac.gov.ae and will notify the First Party of any changes to those documents.
    2. EIAC shall carry out accreditation services consistently in accordance with the relevant International / National standards and Regulations, Guidelines, Requirements of (ARAC, PAC, IAF and ILAC) , EIAC Requirements and avail suitable Personnel to its accreditation service.
    3. EIAC shall only consider objections in writing by CAB to any member of assessment Personnel due to conflict of interest in respect of such member with a clear and valid reasons for the objection within five working days.
    4. In case of unoreseen reasons/ circumstances affecting maintenance of CAB's accreditation; EIAC may but without prejudice to its right to suspend or terminate this Agreement . The suspension will be released once the maintenance of EIAC requirements for Accreditation had been assured.
    5. In the event that accreditation has been granted, accreditation Certificate including the scope shall be issued to the CAB.
    6. EIAC shall publish on its website (www.eiac.gov.ae) , on a regular basis, the status of accredited CABs.
    7. EIAC shall notify the Customer how continuing conformity with the requirements for Accreditation will be monitored in order that it may maintain Accreditation. The frequency of monitoring will be determined by EIAC in accordance with EIAC published documents and will depend on the scope and scale of the Accreditation. In addition to the planned assessment program, EIAC reserves the right to carry out extraordinary visits at any time during the accreditation cycle other than those predeter mined as it may require.
    8. EIAC shall give the CAB Notice immediately upon imposing any kind of sanctions related to the accreditation status of the CAB .
  3. TERMS AND CONDITIONS OF THE AGREEMENT
    1. The agreement shall continue in force unless and until terminated by either party by giving 3 months written notification or if the accreditation is withdrawn.
    2. At the date of termination of this agreement , any certificate of accreditation granted hereunder shall immediately deemed to be invalid.
    3. All out standing charges pursuant to this agreement shall be settled before the termination of this agreement .
    4. In cases of a change in the name of the CAB only, EIAC shall carry out the necessary verifications and subsequent issue of a new accreditation certificate as well as the relative annexes with updated data without there being the need to sign a new agreement .
    5. If the change involves any legal modifications on the status of CAB such as transfer of ownership of CAB; the CAB shall sign a new accreditation agreement .
